Online shopping allows customers to purchase products and services over the internet using websites or mobile applications.
Customers can browse products, compare prices, read reviews, and place orders from their homes.

Most online shopping platforms support digital payment methods such as credit cards, debit cards, net banking, and mobile wallets.
Some platforms also offer cash-on-delivery as a payment option.

Online shopping platforms typically provide order tracking features.
Customers can track shipment status, estimated delivery dates, and delivery progress after placing an order.

Online shopping offers convenience and time savings.
However, it also has disadvantages such as delayed deliveries, limited physical inspection of products, and dependency on internet access.

Online shopping platforms do NOT manufacture products themselves.
They do NOT guarantee product quality beyond seller policies.
They do NOT provide instant delivery in all cases.
